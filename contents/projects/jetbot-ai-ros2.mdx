## How It Works (The Tech Behind It)
To achieve real-time autonomous localization, this system integrates several advanced algorithms and sensors:

* **LIDAR360**: Acts as the "eyes" of the robot, scanning the surrounding environment in 360 degrees to detect obstacles and measure distances accurately.
* **Sensor Fusion (Kalman Filter)**: Reduces noise and uncertainty from the sensors, ensuring the robot always knows its precise speed and direction.
* **Fuzzy Logic**: Implemented to give the robot human-like decision-making capabilities, allowing it to smoothly adjust its speed and avoid obstacles in dynamic environments.
* **Monte Carlo Localization (AMCL)**: The core algorithm that compares the LIDAR scans with a known map to probabilistically determine the exact coordinates of the robot at any given time.

## Challenges & Achievements
One of the biggest challenges in this project was integrating hardware components (shown in the picture) with the **ROS2 middleware** and ensuring the Kalman Filter computations could run smoothly in real-time. The final result is a highly responsive robot capable of localizing itself dynamically with a low margin of error.
